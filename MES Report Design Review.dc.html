TURN 8 · FINAL REVIEW — WHAT SURVIVES CONTACT WITH REALITY
Step back: three pushbacks on our own redesign, then the ship plan
Seven turns produced ~15 components. The design is right; the risk is now execution sequencing and one structural dependency nobody should paper over.
8aThree pushbacks on the redesign itself
1 · The renderer is not the bottleneck — the pipeline is. Half the new components (rank + why-lines, exclusions, opportunity math, the money paragraph, archetype selection) require content the current pipeline doesn't produce. The single worst outcome is beautiful empty opinion slots — a polished template with hedged filler is MORE damning at $1,000 than the current design. Rule: no component ships without its linter rule and degradation path in the same PR, and the renderer refuses to render an unfilled opinion slot (drops the section, logs it) rather than faking one.
2 · Generated opinion is the riskiest surface we added. Why-lines, exclusions and "if this were our money" are LLM judgments; one confidently wrong exclusion ("skip Startmate") that a founder acts on is worse than the old say-everything hedge. Mitigations, non-optional: opinions may only cite evidence inside the report (bounded-claim rule, add as R11); every judgment wears ◐; the money paragraph goes through human review for the first N reports until the register and error rate are known.
3 · Don't build the pretty parts first. The temptation is the Map and logo.dev theming — screenshotable, demo-able. But the rubric shows the gap is trust (evidence 4/15, math 0/15), not glamour. A customer forgives plain typography; they don't forgive an uncited 70%. P0 is genuinely P0.
8bFinal cuts & consolidations
KEEP, unchanged — evidence system with tiers · exec brief + opportunity math · ecosystem directory (7a) · archetype spine · rubric gate · money paragraph.
CONSOLIDATE — Market Entry Map v1 ships as the scorecard fallback only (bars, no scatter): the axis-registry per archetype is real design work and the scatter needs ≥4 well-scored competitors to not look sparse; FINEOS-grade data earns the full map in v2. · 7b dense ledger is not a separate build — same component, density prop.
CUT from v1 — living-document strip (needs refresh infrastructure that doesn't exist; empty "next review" dates would be another intake-apology) · logo.dev theming beyond the cover mark (accent-clamp QA across arbitrary brand colors is a rabbit hole) · TOC per-section verdicts (needs the verdict slots proven first).
One addition — R11 (bounded claims): generated opinions must be traceable to evidence rendered in the same report; the linter rejects opinions citing nothing. This is the rule that makes 1–3 safe.
8cShip plan — two releases, ramped gate
V1 — TRUST RELEASE · GATE ≥ 60
P0 items 1–4 + exec brief + opportunity math + ecosystem directory + scorecard + R1–R11 linter. Acceptance: regenerate FINEOS, CreditLogic and Floats; all three pass the linter with zero manual edits and score ≥60 on the rubric. That regeneration test — not a demo — is the definition of done.
V2 — SIGNATURE RELEASE · GATE ≥ 75
Full Market Entry Map + archetype axis registry · money paragraph out of human review · TOC verdicts · living-document strip · full logo.dev theming. Ships only after ~10 real customer reports have passed v1's gate.
Success metric — not the rubric score (that's the floor): a customer screenshots the exec brief or the money paragraph unprompted. Instrument report shares; that's the number that justifies the price.
